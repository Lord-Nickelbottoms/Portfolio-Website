What makes me, Me.

My favourite Superhero is Spider-Man. Why? Well all Spider-People throughout the Spider-Verse are the type of the people that always persevere regardless of how bleak any situation may seem. A characteristic that I continue to apply when it comes to any task and one that I have gained from playing any of FromSoftware's games. These games teach you that even the smallest of enemies will break you and halt your progress, and happens so often in programming

As a Software Engineer and Gamer, I always find situations in which I face an obstacle and need to apply my learning and skills to overcome the problem to proceed further into the game or project. No matter how much I struggle and face Imposter Syndrome, I am bound to beat the challenge, all it takes is a couple of "Restart from checkpoint" and eventually I'll find a pattern.

One of the major skills a Software Engineer should always have, is Communication. I used to be an extremely shy person who would rather hide in the shadows, but struggling during coding is where I was taught and experienced that communicating one's problem or need for guidance is always a net benefit.

Throughout the years, my learning and experiences have allowed me to build applications by myself and in a team. As cliche as it sounds, I am always looking for learn and grow as a Software Engineer. I am always on the hunt for new challenges and opportunities to expand my knowledge on the nitty and gritty of Software Engineering. Thankfully those came in the form of:

  • ToDoList that was built with Node.JS and MongoDB
  • FlickFndr (a movie database and information display web app) that was built with React
  • ByteBank (a banking application) that was built with React , Java , Springboot and PostgreSQL
  • Student Grade Sorter. Built in COBOL (why is there no logo?)
  • Payroll Processing System (an employee salary calculation System), also built in COBOL
